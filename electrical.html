Mechanical Sidescroller

Electrical

Overview

The electrical subsystem consists of 2 over-arching parts. The first is the power circuitry for our large 12V ~2Amp DC motor. The second is the stepper motor control circuitry. Both are powered from the 12V rail of a 12V/5V wall power supply. This power supply also directly provides power for the raspberryPi from the 5V rail.

The connections within the electrical subsystem

Brushed DC Motor

In order to control the DC motor we use two output pins from the raspberryPi which output unbuffered digital 0V or 3.3V. The first pin outputs a PWM signal used to control velocity of the motor while the second pin outputs 0V for forward and 3.3V for backward. Each of these outputs runs through a sub-circuit to amplify, smooth and buffer the signals. Ultimately the output of the second pin's circuit is used to control a DPDT relay which takes the voltage from the output of the first pin's circuit and switches the direction on the motor accordingly.

The first step for both pins is buffering, which is done simply with an op-amp. For the velocity pin, the pwm signal is smoothed with an RC circuit. The time constant of the RC circuit is approximately 0.1 seconds. Finally, the signal is amplified via a power PMOS transistor in feedback. The resulting voltage is fed into 2 opposite "corners" of the relay, where the other two "corners" are wired to ground. The buffered and amplified signal from the direction pin controls the relay, essentially forming an H-bridge.

Stepper Motor

To move the character up and down, we used a stepper motor and accompanying control circuit (shown below), driven by the GPIO pins on the Raspberry Pi. We buffered the pins with a quad op-amp to avoid frying the pi, then drove the stepper with a quad half-H-bridge.
